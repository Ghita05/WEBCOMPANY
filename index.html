Techniques for Writing Effective Online Help

  • Organize your information for easy navigation: Include access history, a contents menu, a back or home button, and relevant links.
  • Recognize your audience: Write for a balanced level—don’t assume users are either highly technical or not at all.
  • Achieve a positive, personalized tone: Encourage users, especially when they're working on challenging tasks.
  • Design your document: Use color carefully, maintain consistency, choose easy-to-read fonts, and make good use of white space.
  • Be concise: Each screen should focus on one self-contained message.
  • Be clear: Use specific language, provide tutorials, and include pop-up definitions for clarity.
  • Provide access on multiple platforms: Ensure compatibility with various devices and operating systems.
  • Correct your grammar: Poor grammar reduces clarity and damages professionalism.
